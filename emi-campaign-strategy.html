What Drives Conversion — 4 Key Criteria

Every segment in the framework is built on four underlying criteria that directly determine how likely a customer is to book an EMI. Before reading segment performance, it is essential to understand what each of these means and how strongly it moves conversion probability.

01

Previously Booked (PB)

A customer who has converted an Outstanding-to-EMI offer at least once before. Prior booking is the single strongest predictor of future EMI conversion across the entire base.

When a customer has booked an EMI before, they have already cleared the highest barriers: understanding the product, trusting the process, and committing to a repayment structure. The cognitive effort required to book again is dramatically lower. Every additional booking reinforces the habit and deepens familiarity — making previously booked customers the highest-yield group in any campaign.

02

CBOP — Customer Booked Other Product

A customer who has booked any other Creditas product (personal loan, credit limit increase, etc.) in addition to their credit card. Cross-product activity is a strong proxy for financial engagement and buying intent.

Booking another financial product is a meaningful behavioural signal — it tells us the customer is actively engaged with the bank, comfortable making financial decisions, and has already cleared the trust and process barriers. The more products they have booked, and the more recently they booked them, the higher the probability they will convert an EMI offer.

Probability to Book — By CBOP Count & Recency

Absolute conversion probability (i.e. the likelihood a customer actually books) by number of other products booked and recency of the most recent cross-product booking. Base pool average is ~10–12%.

CBOP Count Recency <3 Months Recency 3–6 Months Recency 6M+
1 other product booked 43% 34% 13%
2 other products booked 45% 37% 18%
2+ other products booked 56% 46% 23%
Key Takeaway: A customer with 2+ cross-product bookings in the last 3 months carries a 56% conversion probability — roughly 5× the base average. Recency decay is steep: the same customer at 6M+ recency drops to 23%. The 3-month recency window is critical — act within it or the signal loses most of its power.
03

Churn Vintage — Campaign Fatigue

Churn Vintage (C-score) measures how many times a customer has been contacted in the last 6 months without booking. It is a direct measure of campaign fatigue and a critical modifier of any segment's conversion probability.

The more times a customer is contacted without responding, the more desensitised they become to future outreach. A customer's C-score determines not just how likely they are to convert, but whether it is worth sending to them at all. Ignoring churn vintage inflates send volumes, distorts funnel metrics, and trains customers to tune out — compounding the problem over time.

Conversion Impact by Churn Vintage (C-Score)

C-Score Definition Conversion vs Average Recommended Action
C0 No prior campaign touches this cycle — fresh to outreach 5X Standard cadence. No special treatment needed — naturally high conversion.
C1 – C3 Less than 25% of the Average Churn 3X Continue. Monitor for CBOP or Spike re-activation signal.
C4 – C6 Between 25–50% of average churn 1.5X Refresh channel mix. Introduce offer incentive (reduced fee, cashback).
C7+ Greater than average churn ~0.5X Suppress. Re-enter only on strong re-activation signal (CBOP <3M or Spike2).
The fatigue trap: Every unnecessary send at C4+ actively trains the customer to ignore future communications, making re-activation progressively more expensive. The cost is not just one wasted send — it is a compounding suppression effect.
Re-activation: A C7+ customer who receives a fresh CBOP (<3M) or Spike2 signal should be re-entered with a fully refreshed channel, message, and offer — treated as C0 for that specific trigger event.
04

Balance Spike — Event-Based Trigger

A Spike is a sudden increase in a customer's outstanding balance relative to their 3-month rolling average. Unlike the other criteria, Spike is a real-time event — not a static attribute — creating a time-sensitive window of peak conversion intent.

When a customer sees their outstanding balance jump sharply above normal, the psychological pressure to manage it is at its highest. This is the optimal moment to present an EMI option — the customer is already thinking about their balance, and the offer lands as a solution rather than a sales pitch. Spike campaigns triggered within 24–48 hours of the event consistently outperform all scheduled outreach.

Conversion Impact by Spike Category

Spike Category Balance Increase vs Rolling Avg Conversion vs Average Why It Converts
Spike 3 Below 100% above average 1.5X – 3X Some urgency present, but the balance increase may not feel large enough to prompt immediate action.
Spike 2 ⭐ 100% – 200% above average 3X – 6X The sweet spot. Large enough to create genuine urgency; manageable enough that EMI feels like a real, actionable solution. Highest converter across all segments.
Spike 1 Above 200% above average 4X – 5X Very high urgency, but extreme balances can trigger anxiety or avoidance — slightly lower conversion than Spike 2.
Spike 2 is the sweet spot across all segments. A 100–200% spike creates real pressure without overwhelming the customer. Beyond 200% (Spike 1), some customers mentally "freeze" and defer the decision — reducing conversion despite higher urgency.
Spike amplifies every segment. Any customer — PB, NV, HP, or LP — becomes significantly more responsive during an active Spike event. Campaigns fired within 24–48 hours of the Spike event capture the intent window at its peak.

D2

Pillar 2 — Call Centre Strategy

Two focused roles: (1) recovering customers who dropped off the digital journey, and (2) providing personalised EMI servicing for Previously Booked customers.

ROLE 1 — DROP-OFF RECOVERY
Re-engage customers who initiated the digital EMI journey but did not complete. Time-to-contact is the single most critical variable — every hour of delay halves the conversion probability.
ROLE 2 — PB PERSONALISED SERVICE
Proactive outreach to Previously Booked customers — review existing EMI status, introduce new conversion options, handle inbound queries, and deepen relationship through high-touch banking service.

Same Day 3X 2X Automated re-nudge + CC dial within business hours

Key Insight: A drop-off customer contacted within 1 hour converts at 5X digitally and 4X via call centre. Beyond 10 days, call centre conversion drops to 0.3X — making CC outreach at that stage economically unviable. The playbook is simple: automate the first hour, then escalate to CC within the same day.
