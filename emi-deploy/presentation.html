OSTOEMI -- Outstanding to EMI Conversion

Continuously Refining Segments & Campaign Strategy

Over the last 3 months (Jan -- Mar 2026), we have been continuously refining our segmentation approach and campaign strategy for the IBL OSTOEMI product. Each month brings sharper targeting, better cost efficiency, and improved conversion rates across all segments.

1
Segment Definitions
We have divided our base into 5 key segments
PB
Previously Booked
Accounts that have previously converted an outstanding balance to EMI. Highest propensity to convert again.
NV
New Vintage
Recently acquired accounts with fresh credit history. High engagement potential due to novelty.
LBCL
Last 3 Month LBCL Booked
Accounts that have booked a Loan Below Credit Limit in the last 3 months. Strong signal of credit appetite.
HP
High Propensity
Propensity score 1--4. Model predicts higher likelihood of conversion based on behavioral & transactional signals.
LP
Low Propensity
Propensity score >4. Lower predicted conversion probability but largest volume segment.
Low Churn vs High Churn: We further divide HP and LP into Low Churn (_LC) and High Churn (_Rest) sub-segments based on the amount of campaigning done on the account in the last 6 months. Low Churn accounts have received fewer campaigns and tend to respond better, while High Churn accounts have been heavily campaigned and show fatigue.

PB Segment Deep-dive
For Previously Booked accounts, Propensity and Risk Score do not significantly impact conversion

Key Insight: Propensity & Risk Score Don't Matter for PB

When it comes to Previously Booked (PB) accounts, the propensity model score and MicroFilm risk score have minimal impact on conversion. PB accounts convert at a consistently high rate across all propensity and risk buckets -- their past booking behavior is the strongest predictor of future conversion.
