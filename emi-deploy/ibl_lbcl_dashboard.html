Audience Segment Definitions
High Propensity (Sun, Tue & Wed)
Customers in P1–P4 bands with active loan eligibility. Highest model-predicted likelihood to convert. 3 dedicated sends per week.
45-Day Landed Retarget (Mon & Fri)
Customers who clicked and landed on the offer page 45+ days ago but have not yet booked. Warm intent, requires re-engagement nudge.
Full Base (Thu & Sat)
All eligible customers across all propensity bands and deciles. Broadest reach sends — maximises volume on high-traffic days.
Last 3-Day Drop-offs (Daily)
Customers who landed in the last 1–3 days without booking. Always-on WA Utility flow, suppressed automatically on conversion.
Suppression (Auto)
Customers who have already booked or are in a cooling-off window are automatically excluded from all sends to prevent overcontacting.
Previously Booked (PB)
Customers with a prior LBCL booking — treated as a separate segment. May receive re-engagement only after a defined gap period.
Channel Strategy
Primary Channel
WhatsApp Utility
The default channel for all LBCL campaign sends. WA Utility messages are transactional in nature — they carry the loan offer, CTA link, and personalised loan amount. Utility classification ensures superior deliverability and avoids marketing frequency caps.
High deliverabilityPersonalised CTANo marketing capCost: WA Utility rate
Fallback / Supplementary
SMS L1 / L2, RCS, Email
Used as fallback when WA delivery fails, or as supplementary reach on Full Base days (Thu & Sat). SMS L1 is the primary fallback; L2 covers non-DND opted-in customers. RCS is used for opted-in segments on supported devices. Email is low-priority.
SMS L1 — fallbackSMS L2 — extended reachRCS — rich mediaEmail — passive
