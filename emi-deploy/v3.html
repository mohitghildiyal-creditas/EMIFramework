Framework Overview
The 3 × 3 × 3 Model
3 Criteria · 3 Segments · 3 Customer Tiers — the complete intelligence structure behind every campaign decision
3 Key Criteria — What Drives the P2P Score
CRITERION 01
CBOP
Customer Booked Other Product

What: Customer has booked any other bank product (loan, CLI, insurance) alongside credit card

Why it matters: Cross-product activity doesn't just signal intent — it creates structural financial dependency

Peak signal: 2+ products booked → 2.5× conversion vs base average

CRITERION 02
Churn Vintage
Campaign Fatigue (C-Score)

What: Number of times contacted in last 6 months without booking — direct measure of fatigue

Why it matters: C0 (fresh) → 5× | C7+ (burned out) → ~0.5× — a 10× spread within the same segment

Action: C7+ suppressed unless re-activated by CBOP or Spike event

CRITERION 03
Balance Spike
Real-Time Event Trigger

What: Sudden increase in outstanding balance vs 3-month rolling average — peak psychological urgency

Why it matters: Spike 2 (100–200% above avg) → up to 6× conversion in 24–48h window

Amplifier: Works across ALL segments — even LP customers convert at 2–3× during a spike

3 Segments — Customer Priority Tiers
SEGMENT 01
Repeaters 🏆
Previously Booked — Same Product

Have previously converted an Outstanding-to-EMI offer — cleared all three booking barriers

Convert at 3–5× base average. With Spike2: 6×

#1 Priority — first-touch, top-of-allocation, full channel spend

SEGMENT 02
High Propensity 🎯
High P2P Score — Split by Churn

High intent score from bank's propensity model — but treatment differs sharply by churn history

HP Low Churn → 2.5–3× full spend | HP High Churn → conditional on CBOP/Spike

Cross-product recency <3M or Spike2 unlocks 4×

SEGMENT 03
Low Propensity 💡
60% of Base — Event-Activated

Low propensity score — bulk of the base. Not all low propensity is equal — churn vintage is the differentiator

LP Low Churn → 1.5× low-cost digital | LP High Churn → Suppress (0.15×)

Activated effectively via Drop-off, CIBIL trigger, Discount offers, Voice Bot

Data Ask — Top Priority
Data Requirements — What We Need & Why
Priority data requests to unlock the scoring engine. Items 1–2 are critical for Phase 1 (Repeaters + CBOP). Items 3–5 enable the full P2P model in Phase 2.
TOP PRIORITY
Items 1 and 2 are required immediately to begin Repeater segmentation and Balance Spike detection — the highest-ROI signals in the entire engine.
1
Historical Booking Data — All Products
Enables: Repeater Segment · CBOP Scoring
  • All EMI bookings historically — date, product type, tenure, amount
  • All other product bookings (loans, CLI, insurance, etc.) with date
  • Count of unique products booked per customer (CBOP depth)
⚡ Frequency: One-time historical pull + daily delta updates
2
Daily Outstanding Balance & Spike Detection
Enables: Balance Spike · Event-Based Triggers
  • Daily outstanding balance per account (to compute 3-month rolling average)
  • Credit limit and current utilisation % (for spike normalisation)
  • Statement due date per billing cycle (for due-date proximity scoring)
⚡ Frequency: Daily — spike events must be flagged within 24h
3
Spend Category — All Transactions
Enables: P2P Score Boost · Customer Persona
  • Transaction-level data with MCC / spend category tags (travel, electronics, dining, etc.)
  • Transaction amount and merchant name (for high-ticket TTE identification)
  • Monthly spend frequency and average ticket size (lifestyle signal)
⏱ Frequency: Daily transaction feed — T+1 latency acceptable
4
Internal Risk Score & Bank P2P Score
Enables: HP/LP Classification · Weight 4 in P2P Engine
  • Bank's internal propensity-to-pay score at allocation (Weight 4 in engine)
  • Internal credit/risk score band (drives HP vs LP classification)
  • DPD (days past due) history — last 12 months (for churn and risk calibration)
📅 Frequency: Provided at allocation + refreshed monthly
5
Customer Vintage — Age with the Bank
Enables: New Vintage Segment · Customer Persona Building
  • Date of card issuance / account opening with the bank
  • Date of first transaction (activation vintage)
  • Customer segment / income band as classified by the bank
📋 Frequency: Static field — one-time pull, update on change
6
Others — Secondary Priority
Enables: Full scoring engine enrichment
  • CIBIL Trigger — credit score change event feed (real-time)
  • Regional Data — geography and city tier for persona segmentation
  • Internal Risk & Propensity Score — any additional bank-generated signals
📌 Frequency: As available — secondary data inputs
Priority: Repeater data (item 1) and daily balance data (item 2) unlock the two highest-ROI signals in the engine — Repeaters convert at 3–6× and Spike2 adds a further 1.5× boost on top of any segment. These two feeds alone justify the first phase of scoring investment.
